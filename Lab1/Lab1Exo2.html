Martyrs and massacres : At the time of writing, aerial bombardment, artillery shelling, and gunfire have resulted in 64,232 deaths and 161,583 injuries since the outbreak of the war on October 7 , 2023. The martyrs are classified according to age group and gender as follows: 19,424 child martyrs, representing 30.2%, including 400 infants born and martyred during the war, 10,138 female martyrs, representing 15.8%, including more than 8,150 mothers,4,695 elderly martyrs, representing 7.3%, 29,975 martyrs, men and youth, representing 46.7%, According to the categories of work with privacy as follows: 1590 martyrs from medical staff,122 civil defense martyrs, 249 martyred journalists, 171 municipal employees were martyred, including 4 mayors, 778 martyrs from the sports and scouting movement.

At the level of families and collective groups, official statistics indicate that: More than 38,000 families were subjected to Israeli massacres, More than 2,613 families were wiped out and erased from the civil registry (a total of 7,563 martyrs), More than 5,943 families were wiped out, leaving only one survivor (a total of 11,901 martyrs), Finally, figures relating to the starvation policy in recent months indicate: Two thousand and 356 people have been killed and more than 17,244 injured as they search for a living at the US- and Israeli-backed Gaza Humanitarian Foundation centers since they opened on May 27,370 martyrs, including 131 children, as a result of famine and malnutrition.

annihilation of life : The Israeli war of extermination has systematically and unprecedentedly targeted 15 vital sectors, causing massive destruction. According to the Director General of the Government Media Office, Dr. Ismail Al-Thawabtah, the total cost of direct initial losses in these sectors exceeds $68 billion.
The occupation targeted health facilities and equipment with shelling, destroying or putting them out of service, according to the following figures: 38 hospitals,96 healthcare centers,147 ambulances,55 civil defense vehicles (rescue and firefighting), The healthcare sector's losses are estimated at approximately $5 billion, including the operation of hospitals, health centers, radiology centers, medicines and pharmacies, and the costs of surgical procedures in the private and public sectors, among others.
Education sector: This sector has suffered a significant share of destruction, with universities and schools being targeted, teachers, school principals, faculty members, scholars, and university professors being killed, and tens of thousands of students being martyred. The occupation has destroyed 156 schools, universities and educational institutions in total ,382 schools, universities and educational institutions partially. Losses to the education sector and academic institutions are estimated at approximately $3.5 billion, including the destruction of buildings and facilities, equipment, laboratories, and educational equipment for medical and engineering colleges, as well as classrooms, laboratories, and sports facilities

industrial sector : The industrial sector has been devastated by the targeting of various factories and industrial establishments. This has brought all types of factories to a standstill, bringing production to a complete standstill. This has deepened the suffering of citizens due to the severe shortage of various goods and commodities in the markets. Initial losses are estimated at approximately $4 billion.
Commercial sector: The commercial sector, including businesses, banks, and exchange offices, bore a heavy share of the destruction. This sector includes markets, shops, restaurants, hotels, commercial warehouses, and other facilities. Initial losses are estimated at approximately $4.3 billion.
government sector: The occupation destroyed 236 government headquarters, including ministries, institutions, and government agencies. Due to the war, government work in general has come to a standstill, disrupting citizens' interests and halting economic revenues to an unprecedented degree, both from citizens and from the government. The financial exchange process that drives the local market in general has also come to a standstill, with financial losses to this sector estimated at approximately two billion dollars.
domestic sector: The initial direct losses in this sector include the destruction of all the contents of residential units, all homes, towers, and buildings, along with everything they contain, including clothing, furniture, electrical appliances, kitchens, their furnishings, and various household appliances. The losses are estimated at approximately $4 billion.
